Architecture of the World

Showcasing the different styles of architecture around the world.

Greek Architecture

Example Photo Description Key Points
Greek architecture is comprised mainly of simple, realistic, and harmonious buildings. Greek architectural examples are temples, theatres, and stadiums. Later on during the reign of the Roman Empire, there would be a period called the Hellenistic period where architects drew heavy inspiration from Greek culture. This period would forever etch Greek architecture into the world from the Renaissance to the modern day.
  • Simple
  • Realistic
  • Hellenistic Period

Chinese Architecture

Example Photo Description Key Points
The Chinese primarily used wood for everything as it was a heavily abundant resource, and it was useful against floods and other seismic phenomena as the structure was stamped to the ground and had pillars that were not embedded into the foundation which allowed flexibility incase of any damages. They also had overhanging ceramic tiles as their roof which protected the building from getting wet easily as wood rots faster when exposed to water.
  • Practical
  • Wooden
  • Overhanging Roof

Gothic Architecture

Example Photo Description Key Points
Named after the Goths, a Germanic group that fought against the Romans, Gothic architecture came after the fall of the Roman empire, and was first used by the French to escape from the Romanesque style. This style of architecture uses pointed arches, elaborate stained glass mosaics, and ornaments such as gargoyles. One famous example would be the Cologne Cathedral in Germany which has been in construction for many centuries, having been completed in the 1900s.
  • Un-Romanesque
  • Pointed Objects
  • Ornamental Objects

Victorian Architecture

Example Photo Description Key Points
Named after Queen Victoria of England, Victorian architecture was inspired by most of the other styles in Europe; it took inspiration from Gothic, French, Italian, Tudor, and even Egyptian architecture. Victorian homes were usually quite tall to look imposing, typically 2-3 stories at least. they also had complicated shapes to break away from the static Greek revival style. These structures could include a large porch with ornaments and the homes were painted in bright, vibrant colors.
  • Combined European Influences
  • Imposing
  • Vibrant Colors

Filipino Architecture

Example Photo Description Key Points
Initially, we built our homes out of the resources in the environment which is named "Bahay Kubo." After being colonized by the Spanish, we moved to the "Antillian" architecture which was adapted into a Filipino style. We had "Bahay-na-Bato" or stone houses which is a blend of Filipino and Spanish architecture. The Spanish also influenced the construction of churches in the Philippines, like the San Agustin church. When the Americans came, they brought over Steel and concrete, turning the Philippines into a neoclassical-esque style.
  • Bahay Kubo
  • Bahay na Bato
  • American Neoclassicalism
